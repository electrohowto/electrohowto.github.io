The Integrated Circuit (IC)

An integrated circuit, or IC, is a device that combines many discrete components into a single chip.

The symbol for a 555 timer IC looks like this:

ICs come in a wide variety of styles, from simple timers, to seven-segment decoders, to programmable microcontrollers.

Many circuits can be built using ICs, including clocks, thermometers, and random number generators.

Without ICs, we would still be using transistors for our logic!

For our main classes of ICs, there are two classes...

The 7400 Series

These ICs use BJT transistors as the base for their logic, hence "transistor-transistor logic" (TTL).

The voltage on these ICs must not drop below or exceed 5V.

The 4000 Series

These ICs use MOSFETs as the base of their logic, hence "complementary metal oxide semiconductor" (CMOS).

Their voltages must not exceed 15V. They're also sensitive to static electricity.
