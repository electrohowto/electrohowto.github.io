The Vacuum Fluorescent Display (VFD)

A vacuum fluorescent display, or VFD, is a display device that uses the same principles of a triode vacuum tube to light phosphor-coated anode plates.

The symbol for a single-digit VFD looks like this:

A VFD consists of an evacuated glass tube, a cathode comprised of a tungsten filament, a grid (or a few on multiple digit VFDs), and anode plates for the segments.

To control a VFD tube with a seven-segment decoder IC, on 5V you'll need transistors to drive the 12V signals.

Do not connect the cathode to 12V; you'll burn it out. Connect it to 3V, preferably AC. DC will cause a gradient in the display.

Multiplexing VFDs requires grid control. First one grid turns on, then the next, and so on, at a rapid speed. This also requires higher grid and anode voltage, at least 20V is needed for the VFD to work.
