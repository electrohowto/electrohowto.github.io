The LED

An LED, or light-emitting diode, is a component that emits light when a small voltage and current passes through it.

The symbol for an LED looks like this:

LEDs come in many different colors, from the classic red, to ultraviolet. Multicolor LEDs exist, too.

LEDs have a wide variety of uses, from a power on indicator to a pixel in a billboard.

To light an LED, you can use a simple 3V coin cell. But if you were to light an LED using a 9V battery, you would need a resistor to limit the current across the LED to prevent permanent damage.

If running an LED off of high voltages (140V for example), you'd need a power resistor big enough to handle the energy loss. A quarter-watt resistor would burn up in this application.
