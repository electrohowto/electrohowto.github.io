Safety Tips and Warnings

Safety is the top priority in building a circuit.

Without following these safeguards, you could risk fatal injuries!

High Voltage

Circuits with high voltage, such as Nixie boost converter circuits that output up to 200V DC, are a big safety concern in the electronics world.

Here's a Flash program showing a person's two fingers touching a 200V constant supply.

Did the scream of agony convince you not to touch high voltage lines? If not, here's the warning again in big, bold letters...

DO NOT TOUCH HIGH VOLTAGE LINES!

Hot/Exploding Components

In the event you happen to pass too high of voltage (or current) through a component, or connect it in reverse, do not grab it! Immediately power off the device.

If you grab the component that is connected incorrectly or to too much voltage or current, you could very easily injure yourself from a burn or component shrapnel.

Soldering

When soldering, there are a few safeguards to follow:

  1. The tip reaches temperatures exceeding 350°C (660°F), so do not touch the tip unless you want a severe burn.
  2. You do not want to breathe the fumes from the solder. It is poisonous!
  3. Finally, make sure all power is turned off before soldering, especially with high voltage circuits.

Short Circuits

Avoid these at all time! They'll wear on your power supply and components, and worse yet, destroy them completely.
