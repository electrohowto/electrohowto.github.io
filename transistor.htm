The Transistor

A transistor acts as a switch in a circuit. It can be used to amplify signals, or to switch a signal on or off.

There are two types of transistors: NPN and PNP. The symbols for the two types of transistors look like this:

Transistors come in every shape and size imaginable, ranging from the common TO-92 package to the hefty TO-3 package.

You can find transistors in any digital circuit out there! Some even come with two in one package; these are known as Darlington transistors.

Another type of transistor exists, too: the field-effect transistor (FET). These are different from standard bipolar junction transistors (BJTs) in that they're unipolar, and that their gates are voltage driven rather than current driven.

MOSFETs are a type of FET that can be driven up to 15V on the gate, rather than the 5V of the base of a transistor.

When working with BJTs in a 12V circuit, it is wise to use a 100K resistor on the base to avoid destroying the internal diode from overcurrent.

MOSFETs will work fine on 12V. Be sure not to bring the MOSFET near any static electricity, though, or you will destroy the semiconductor.
