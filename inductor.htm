The Inductor

An inductor is a coil of wire that impedes the flow of electric current. The unit of measure for impedence is the henry (H).

The symbol for an inductor coil looks like this:

There are different types of inductive components, such as basic coils, solenoids, electromagnets, motors, transformers, and relays.

You'll find inductor coils in power supplies. They aid in smoothing out AC ripple.

When controlling an inductor via a switch or transistor, you must include a diode connected in reverse to prevent a voltage spike from destroying your switching device. This diode is known as a "flyback diode".
