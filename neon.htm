The Neon Lamp

A neon lamp uses neon gas to light a cathode inside a glass bulb.

The symbol for a neon lamp looks like this:

Neon lamps, unlike LEDs, require a high voltage to light. They require at least 65V AC, or 90V DC, for the plain neon lamps. Colored neon lamps require higher voltages. See the safeguard page for information on working with high voltage.

You'll find neon lamps in some older equipment as power indicators, and in most 120V illuminated power switches.

You can make your own night light using a neon lamp and a 100K resistor.

When working with neon lamps, on DC you'll notice only one cathode lights out of two. On AC, both cathodes light.
