The Nixie Tube

A Nixie tube is a numerical display device that uses the same principles of a neon lamp to light numeric-shaped cathodes.

The symbol for a Nixie tube looks like this:

And here's a real-life example, a GN-4:

Like neon lamps, Nixie tubes require high voltages to operate. They operate at voltages between 170V and 200V DC. See the safeguard page for information on working with high voltage.

Nixie tubes aren't used in much new equipment, but can be found in a plethora of old test equipment from the 1960s-70s.

Nowadays, clocks are built around this humble tube.

Don't forget the 20K resistor on the anode. You don't want to destroy the tube!

If you reverse the polarity on a tube, the anode grid will glow instead of a number.
