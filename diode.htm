The Diode

A diode acts as a one-way check valve in a circuit. It allows current to flow one way and blocks reverse current flow.

The symbol for a diode looks like this:

To demonstrate the properties of a diode, let's have a look at this interactive Flash program...

It may seem like it just clips a negative voltage in an AC signal, however, that's not the case... a part of that signal is lost due to a 0.7V drop on the diode.

Also, be sure to run current at or below the limit, otherwise you will destroy the diode!

Diodes aren't just for blocking reverse current. They can be used to make ROM boards, too.

If arranged in a matrix, diodes can carry information. A diode is a "1" and a blank spot on the board is a "0".
